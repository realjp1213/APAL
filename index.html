How it works

Feeling anxious? Whether something is about to happen, is happening now, or already happened, APAL helps you work through it in 3 simple steps.

1. Describe the situation

Tell APAL what happened and what’s on your mind.

2. Challenge the thought

Use guided CBT questions to review it.

3. Track your progress

Set a goal and build confidence over time.

The Power of CBT

~7 in 10 people who use CBT techniques report meaningful improvement in anxiety.

Problem?

Most CBT worksheets are printed, easy to lose, or hard to use in the moment… which is when many social situations actually happen.

APAL brings these tools to your phone.

Instead of filling out worksheets by hand, you can use APAL to work through anxious thoughts, save your entries, and track progress over time.

Built with guidance from clinical psychologists

Before a social moment

Jenny is worried about a dinner with her colleagues. She keeps imagining awkward moments, thinking she’ll have nothing interesting to say, and fearing people will see her as boring.

Before a social moment

When she opens APAL, she writes down her fears and works through a short CBT exercise. APAL helps her question the idea that everyone will judge her, and guides her toward a small, realistic step for the evening.

Before a social moment

After doing the exercise, Jenny feels less overwhelmed and more ready for the dinner. She still feels nervous, but the fear no longer controls the whole picture.

During a social moment

Andrew is at a party. He starts feeling awkward and notices his mind racing: “I don’t know what to say,” “People can tell I’m uncomfortable,” “I should leave.”

During a social moment

He steps aside, opens APAL, and follows a quick grounding exercise. Then he writes down the thoughts making him spiral. APAL helps him test those thoughts with simple CBT questions.

During a social moment

Andrew saves the moment in his Entries so he can reflect on it later. He goes back to the party with a more open approach and starts his first conversation of the night.

After a social moment

Ron had a quick chat with his neighbours. As soon as he got home, he started replaying the moment again and again and assuming he sounded strange.

After a social moment

When he found APAL, he finally wrote down the thoughts bothering him. APAL helped him question those assumptions and suggested a small step for the next time he sees them.

After a social moment

Ron used to get stuck in rumination for days. After using APAL for a while, he’s learned to slow down these thoughts, review them, and move forward instead of staying stuck.
